Textual Analysis - GarmentIQ Case Study

Scenario: Manager logs in, reviews operations, and notifies supplier

UC-01 - Log in to system Sarah Mitchell (Manager) opens the GarmentIQ web portal and enters her credentials (sarah.mitchell@gmail.com, username garment-iq-sarah1991). The system validates her identity against the USERS table, confirms her role_id = 1 (Manager) from the ROLES table, and grants her full access to the dashboard and all management modules.

UC-02 - View dashboard Upon login, Sarah lands on the manager dashboard. The system queries the SALES, PRODUCTS, and INVENTORY_LOG tables to display key metrics: total revenue this week, top-selling products, and current low-stock alerts. She notices that the "Dark Blue Hoodie (Size M)" has fallen below its reorder_level of 25 units.

MUC-01 - Manage suppliers Sarah navigates to the Suppliers module. The system retrieves all records from the SUPPLIERS table. She identifies that the responsible supplier for hoodies is Sakura Textiles Co. (supplier_id = 1, Japan). She reviews their contact details - kenji.yamamoto@sakuratextiles.jp - and notes the last production order placed with them.

MUC-02 - Manage factories Sarah cross-references the FACTORIES table and confirms that Sakura Stitch Factory in Osaka, Japan (factory_id = 1) is linked to Sakura Textiles. She checks its current active production orders via the PRODUCTION_ORDERS table and sees two orders with status "In Progress."

MUC-03 - Create production order Sarah creates a new production order for 200 units of the Dark Blue Hoodie. The system inserts a new record into the PRODUCTION_ORDERS table, referencing product_id from PRODUCTS and factory_id = 1. The order status is set to "Pending" and start/end dates are assigned.

MUC-04 - Notify supplier (automated email) The system detects the new production order and triggers an automated notification email to kenji.yamamoto@sakuratextiles.jp. The email includes order details: product SKU (GIQ-XXXX), quantity (200 units), colour (Dark Blue), size (M), and the required delivery date. This closes the loop between the PRODUCTION_ORDERS and SUPPLIERS tables.

SUC-01 - Manage inventory (Staff perspective) Meanwhile, Staff member John Doe (johndoe@gmail.com, role_id = 3) logs in. He accesses the Inventory module and updates the stock count for three products - adjusting quantities in the PRODUCTS table (stock_quantity field). Each change is automatically written to the INVENTORY_LOG table with change_type = ADJUSTMENT, recording stock_before, stock_after, the user_id, and a timestamp.

SUC-02 - Record sale A customer, Ella Brown (cstm26-0042), purchases a Blood Red T-Shirt (Size S). John records the sale - the system inserts into the SALES table referencing product_id, customer_id = cstm26-0042, and user_id of John. The stock_quantity in PRODUCTS decreases by 1, and a new INVENTORY_LOG entry is created with change_type = SALE.

SUC-03 - Manage customers The system detects that cstm26-0042 is a returning customer (already exists in CUSTOMERS table). John can view Ella's purchase history, address, and email. If it were a new customer, John would create a new record in CUSTOMERS with a new cstm26-XXXX ID.

AUC-01 - View reports & analytics (Analyst perspective) Analyst James Nguyen logs in and accesses the analytics module. He exports data from SALES, PRODUCTS, CUSTOMERS, and INVENTORY_LOG to Excel. He loads it into Power BI and builds dashboards showing monthly revenue trends by category, top customers by spend, and factory production efficiency vs. orders placed.
